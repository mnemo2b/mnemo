# Research: Habit Formation

## Question

What does the research say about how habits actually form, and what parts of the pop-science narrative hold up?

## "21 days to form a habit"

Wrong. Origin: Maxwell Maltz, *Psycho-Cybernetics* (1960), observations about amputees adjusting to prosthetics — not habit research.

Actual research (Lally et al., 2010): median time to automaticity for a simple habit was 66 days; range 18-254 days. Complex habits take substantially longer.

## Habit loop (Duhigg / Charles Duhigg)

- **Cue** — trigger
- **Routine** — behavior
- **Reward** — reinforcement

Close enough, but underspecified. The actual cognitive science version is operant conditioning + implementation intentions + context-dependent memory. Duhigg's model is a useful shorthand, not the mechanism.

## What actually works

- **Implementation intentions** (Gollwitzer) — "if [situation], then [behavior]" statements pre-commit your response. Evidence is strong across many behaviors.
- **Context stability** — habits formed in one context don't transfer easily. Moving, travel, schedule change disrupts habits.
- **Small enough to fail-proof** — starting with a version so small you can't skip ("put on running shoes") builds the cue-routine pair
- **Consistency > intensity** — missing a day occasionally doesn't reset the "streak" neurologically
- **Rewards close to the behavior** — immediate > delayed

## What doesn't work as claimed

- **"Visualization"** — actually decreases goal pursuit; you get the reward of imagining, less drive to do
- **"Will power / discipline"** — finite resource framing is now contested; motivation is partially context-dependent
- **Public accountability** — mixed; depends on person and specific behavior
- **Tracking apps** — help for some people, hurt for others (reduction to numbers crowds out intrinsic motivation)

## Breaking habits

Harder than building. Cue remains; replacement routine needed. Or: environmental modification (remove the cue entirely).

## Sources

- Wendy Wood — *Good Habits, Bad Habits*; research on habitual behavior
- Peter Gollwitzer — implementation intentions
- Lally et al. 2010 — automaticity timing
- BJ Fogg — *Tiny Habits*, useful tactics despite overclaims
- Duhigg — *The Power of Habit*, useful shorthand despite pop framing

## My takeaway

Start small. Anchor to existing behaviors. Design the environment to make the cue inevitable. Expect 2-3 months, not 3 weeks. Context stability is underrated — big life changes disrupt everything.
