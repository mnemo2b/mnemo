# Research: Python (snake) Dietary Requirements

## Question

What does the reptile husbandry literature say about long-term ball python (Python regius) feeding? Current consensus on frequency, prey size, variety?

## Scope

Wild diet vs captive diet — often divergent. Captive recommendations draw from breeder observation, not controlled studies. Few peer-reviewed nutrition studies exist for P. regius specifically.

## Wild diet (sources)

- Ball pythons are dietary specialists — primarily small rodents, occasionally birds
- Luiselli & Angelici (1998) field study in Nigeria — rodents ~95% of stomach contents
- Prey size correlates with snake size; juveniles take smaller rodents than adults
- Feeding frequency unknown with precision — likely 1-2 prey/month on average, highly variable

## Captive practice (observed)

- **Adults**: medium-large rat every 10-21 days
- **Juveniles**: small rat every 7-10 days
- **Hatchlings**: pinky or fuzzy every 5-7 days
- **Prey size**: ~10-15% body weight is standard guidance

## Frequency: competing views

- **Power feeding** (more frequent, faster growth) — common in breeding stock, linked to obesity and reduced longevity
- **Maintenance feeding** (every 14-21 days for adults) — current best practice for pet snakes
- **Seasonal variation** — wild snakes fast more in winter; captive snakes can mimic, though unnecessary for pet-level husbandry

## Variety

Debated:
- Some keepers rotate mice and rats; nutritional justification weak
- Others feed only rats; works fine long-term
- Pre-killed frozen-thawed rodents are the default and well-tolerated

## Supplementation

- Whole prey provides calcium (bones) and vitamins (organs); typically no supplementation needed
- Exception: long-term F/T feeders sometimes get gut-loaded prey or dusted items, rare consensus that it matters

## Open questions

- Long-term effects of captive overfeeding on kidney and liver
- Whether variety of prey improves outcomes
- Optimal fasting cycles mimicking wild patterns
- Evidence-based upper limit on feeding frequency
