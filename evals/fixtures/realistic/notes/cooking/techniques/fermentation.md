# Fermentation

Controlled microbial activity that transforms food. Lacto-fermentation (vegetables, hot sauce), wild yeast (sourdough), cultured (kombucha, yogurt).

## Lacto-ferments

- 2% salt by weight of vegetables + water
- Submerge under brine
- Anaerobic environment (airlock or weight)
- 3-10 days at room temp, then cold storage

Good for: cabbage (sauerkraut, kimchi), carrots, cauliflower, hot peppers, garlic.

## Signs of life

- Brine turns cloudy
- Smell is sour, not rotten
- Bubbles
- Taste evolves from salty → tangy → funky

## Troubleshooting

- **Mold on surface** — toss it. White film (kahm yeast) is usually ok, scrape off.
- **Too salty** — cut salt next time, not this one
- **Mushy** — ferment was too warm or too long
- **No bubbles after 5 days** — too cold, or the vegetables were anti-microbial (lots of garlic)

## Notes

Fermented hot sauce is worth the wait. Blend after 2-3 weeks, strain, season. Keeps a year refrigerated.
