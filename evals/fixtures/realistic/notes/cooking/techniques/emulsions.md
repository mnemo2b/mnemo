# Emulsions

Two liquids that don't want to mix, held together by an emulsifier. Vinaigrettes, mayonnaise, hollandaise, beurre blanc.

## Formula

- **Dispersed phase** — droplets (usually oil or butter)
- **Continuous phase** — surrounding liquid (water, vinegar, stock)
- **Emulsifier** — molecule with hydrophilic and hydrophobic ends (egg yolk lecithin, mustard, garlic paste)

## Mayo

- 1 yolk per 200ml oil max
- Room temp ingredients
- Drizzle oil slowly while whisking — speed of incorporation matters
- If it breaks: add cold water and whisk, or start over with fresh yolk and drizzle broken mayo in

## Vinaigrette

- 3:1 oil to vinegar
- Mustard or honey stabilizes
- Shake hard in a jar, or whisk with ice to blast it together

## Beurre blanc

- Reduce white wine + shallot to 2 tbsp
- Off heat, whisk in cold butter cubes one at a time
- Never boil — butter will break

## Why emulsions break

- Too fast addition
- Temperature shock
- Overwhelmed emulsifier
