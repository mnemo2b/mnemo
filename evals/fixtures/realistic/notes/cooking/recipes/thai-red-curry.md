# Thai Red Curry

Red curry (gaeng ped) is made with dried red chilies, which gives it a deeper, earthier, slightly smoky character compared to green curry.

## Paste

- dried red chilies (soaked), deseeded for less heat
- galangal, lemongrass, makrut lime zest
- shallots, garlic
- white pepper, shrimp paste
- toasted cumin, coriander seed

Soaking the dried chilies is critical — dry chili in the pound becomes bitter and powdery.

## Curry

1. Fry paste in coconut cream until oil separates
2. Add protein — typically duck, beef, or pork for red curry
3. Thin with coconut milk and stock
4. Simmer 20-30 min
5. Add vegetables in the last few minutes
6. Season with fish sauce, palm sugar, a splash of tamarind
7. Finish with Thai basil

## Red vs green

- **Red** — dried chili, earthier, pairs with red meat and heartier veg
- **Green** — fresh chili, brighter, pairs with chicken/fish and tender veg
- Both use the same base aromatics (galangal, lemongrass, makrut lime)
