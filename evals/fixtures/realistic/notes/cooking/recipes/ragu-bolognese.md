# Ragù Bolognese

Slow-cooked meat sauce from Bologna. The authentic version uses milk and wine, and it's served on tagliatelle, not spaghetti.

## Ingredients

- 400g ground beef (80/20)
- 200g ground pork
- 150g pancetta, diced fine
- 1 large onion, celery stalk, carrot — all finely diced (soffritto)
- 250ml whole milk
- 250ml dry white wine
- 400g canned san marzano tomatoes, crushed
- salt, pepper, nutmeg

## Steps

1. Render pancetta, then sweat the soffritto until soft
2. Brown the meat hard — you want fond on the bottom of the pot
3. Deglaze with wine, reduce until almost dry
4. Add milk, simmer until mostly absorbed (this tenderizes the meat)
5. Add tomatoes, simmer uncovered 2-3 hours on lowest heat
6. Finish with a pinch of nutmeg

## Notes

Don't rush the milk step — it's what separates a good ragù from a great one. Serve on fresh egg tagliatelle.
