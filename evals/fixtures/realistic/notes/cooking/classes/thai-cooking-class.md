# Thai Cooking Class — Chiang Mai, 2025

Full day at a family farm outside Chiang Mai. Started at the market, finished with six dishes on the table. Instructor was Nok — second generation cook.

## Morning: Market tour

- **Basil** — Thai basil (horapa), holy basil (kraphao), lemon basil (manglak). All different.
- **Chili varieties** — bird's eye (small, hot), prik chi fa (longer, milder), dried red for curry paste
- **Coriander** — the ROOTS are the flavor, not leaves. Leaves are garnish.
- **Galangal vs ginger** — galangal is harder, more citrusy, not interchangeable
- **Palm sugar** — balances fish sauce; buy fresh, not powdered
- **Makrut lime** — zest the peel, leaves go in late

## Afternoon: Cooking

### Green curry paste (from scratch, mortar)

- Pound in order: hardest first (galangal, lemongrass), then aromatics, then herbs
- ~25 min of sustained pounding
- You want a paste, not a chop

### Tom kha gai

- Coconut milk + galangal + lemongrass + makrut lime leaves
- Chicken poaches in seasoned broth, NOT boiled
- Fish sauce + lime at the end, off heat

### Pad thai

- Soak noodles in room-temp water until pliable
- Sauce: tamarind + palm sugar + fish sauce, reduced to syrup
- Wok must be smoking hot before anything hits it
- Egg goes in at the end, pushed to the side to set

### Mango sticky rice

- Steam glutinous rice (not boil)
- Coconut sauce: coconut milk + palm sugar + pinch of salt, reduced

## Takeaways

- Balance: salty (fish sauce) + sweet (palm sugar) + sour (lime) + spicy (chili) in every dish
- Fresh aromatics transform results — no substitutes
- A good mortar is the most important Thai kitchen tool
- Taste constantly, adjust constantly
