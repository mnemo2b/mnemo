# Cookware

Minimum viable kitchen:

## Essentials

- **12" carbon steel skillet** — sears, fries, everything non-delicate. Seasoned properly, it's non-stick.
- **10" cast iron** — indoor-to-oven, bread, cornbread
- **5.5qt dutch oven** (Le Creuset or Staub) — braises, stews, bread, deep fry
- **8qt stock pot** — pasta, stock, big batches
- **Saucepan** (2qt, stainless tri-ply) — sauces, rice, small portions
- **Non-stick 10"** (cheap, replaceable) — eggs only. Replace every 2-3 years.
- **Sheet pans** (half and quarter) — roasting, cookies, everything
- **Wok** (carbon steel) — stir fry, deep fry, steaming

## Don't need

- Copper cookware (expensive, high-maintenance, marginal gain)
- Ceramic "healthy" coatings (they wear out fast)
- Matching sets (mix for best tool per job)

## Care

- Carbon steel / cast iron: never soap-wash aggressively, dry immediately, light oil
- Stainless: Bar Keeper's Friend is magic
- Non-stick: wooden tools only, never over high heat
