# Kitchen Knives

Current rotation:

- **8" chef's knife** (Tojiro DP, VG10) — daily driver for 90% of tasks
- **6" petty** (Tojiro) — small hands-on work, fruit, garlic
- **Bread knife** (Wüsthof) — serrated, long enough for boules
- **Honing steel** — use before each session
- **Whetstones** — 1000/6000 combo, for sharpening every few months

## What I don't need

- Santoku (duplicates chef's knife)
- Cleaver (rarely used, heavy)
- Filet knife (only if you fish regularly)
- Nakiri (nice but 8" chef covers the use case)

## Buying guide

- Japanese steel holds an edge longer; German steel is more forgiving
- Full tang for balance
- Weight matters — heft it before buying
- Avoid dishwasher-safe claims (they ruin edges)

## Care

- Hand wash, dry immediately
- Wood or plastic boards only
- Hone before every session, sharpen quarterly
- Store on a magnetic strip or in a block, never loose in a drawer
