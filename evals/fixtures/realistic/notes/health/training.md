# Training

Current approach. Simple, sustainable, compounding.

## Structure

- **4 days / week** — two upper, two lower, full-body focus
- **1 hr sessions** — compound lifts first, accessories after
- **Double progression** — add reps in a range, then add weight when range is maxed
- **Deload every 4-6 weeks** — dropping volume 40% for a week

## Lifts

- Squat (low bar)
- Deadlift (conventional, RDLs as accessory)
- Bench press (flat, paused)
- Overhead press (standing)
- Pull-ups and rows
- Accessories: curls, tricep work, face pulls, carries

## Cardio

- 2-3 easy zone-2 sessions per week, 30-45 min (walk, bike, row)
- 1 HIIT session max, alternating weeks
- Not training for a specific event — cardio is for health, not performance

## Recovery

- Sleep (8 hr target, see `notes/health/sleep.md`)
- Protein 1.6-2.2g/kg bodyweight
- At least 1 full rest day per week
- Take more when sick or stressed; gains don't happen in the gym

## What I've learned

- Consistency > intensity (80% effort for a year > 100% for 6 weeks)
- Form breakdown is a sign to stop, not push
- Track lifts (notes app, spreadsheet, whatever) — memory lies
- Listen to pain signals: novel = stop. Familiar soreness = continue.
