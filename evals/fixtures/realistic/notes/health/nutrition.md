# Nutrition

Practical daily eating. Not a diet; not a philosophy. Just what works.

## Building blocks

- **Protein every meal** — 30-50g per meal, 3-4 meals a day
- **Mostly whole foods** — things that look like what they came from
- **Vegetables take up real plate space** — not a garnish
- **Fats are not the enemy** — olive oil, nuts, oily fish, whole eggs
- **Hydration** — 2.5-3L water; thirst is a late signal

## What I eat most days

- Breakfast: eggs + toast + fruit, or overnight oats + yogurt + berries
- Lunch: salad with a big protein on top, lots of dressing
- Snack: nuts, fruit, cottage cheese, or a protein shake after training
- Dinner: protein + grain + two veg; varies by mood

## 80/20

80% whole foods, 20% whatever you want. Works better than rigid restriction long-term. Going out for a great meal is worth it.

## What's worth being careful about

- Ultra-processed snacks (the packaging strategy is to make you eat more)
- Liquid calories (juice, sugary coffee, frequent alcohol)
- "Healthy" snacks with 200+ cal/serving (nuts, granola — easy to overdo)

## What's not worth fretting about

- Organic vs conventional (real impact is specific foods, not everything)
- Specific timing (pre/post workout within reason)
- Demonizing single foods (eggs, red meat, coffee — the research is nuanced)
