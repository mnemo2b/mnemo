# Sleep

What's moved the needle after years of tracking.

## Cornerstones

- **Consistent wake time** — same time every day, weekends included
- **Sunlight within 1 hr of waking** — 10 min outside, no sunglasses
- **Cool bedroom** — 17-19°C
- **Dark bedroom** — blackout curtains, or an eye mask
- **No caffeine after noon** — half-life is longer than you think

## Hygiene, ranked by impact

1. Wake time consistency
2. Daily sunlight + movement
3. Room temperature
4. Light exposure before bed (screens, bright lights)
5. Caffeine timing
6. Last meal timing (3-4 hr before bed)
7. Alcohol (destroys REM)

## What doesn't matter as much as you think

- Specific hours (7 vs 8 is personal)
- Mattress brand (support + comfort, don't overthink)
- Supplements (magnesium and tart cherry juice are the only ones with evidence; benefits are small)

## What I track

- Wake time consistency (± 30 min target)
- Time in bed vs sleep (efficiency > 85%)
- Subjective energy at 3pm (the dip)

Tracking with a ring (Oura / Whoop / Apple Watch) is motivating but not strictly necessary. A notebook works.

## Recovery from bad nights

- Don't compensate with a long nap (< 20 min if you must)
- Get sunlight + movement as early as possible
- Early to bed the next night, not excessively so (throws rhythm)
