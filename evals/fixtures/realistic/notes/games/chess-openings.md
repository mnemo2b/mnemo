# Chess Openings

My opening repertoire. Casual player (~1400 blitz), aiming for solid positions without memorizing 20 moves.

## As white

- **1. e4** — simpler structures, faster development
- Against e5: Ruy Lopez (main line, Berlin defense if I'm tired)
- Against c5: Open Sicilian, Najdorf as main line to learn
- Against e6: Advance French, avoid main line theory
- Against c6: Exchange Caro-Kann — simple plans

## As black

- **vs 1. e4**: Caro-Kann — solid, strategic, few tactical minefields
- **vs 1. d4**: Nimzo-Indian, or Queen's Indian against Nf3

## Rapid / blitz pragmatism

- Memorize 5-8 moves deep in your main lines
- Know the plans after theory ends, not just the moves
- Have backup lines when opponents go offbeat
- Endgame practice matters more than opening depth at club level

## What I stopped doing

- Switching openings constantly — diffuses learning across too many lines
- Memorizing sidelines I never see
- Watching opening videos without testing positions afterward
- Playing hyper-theoretical lines without the willingness to study daily

## Practice

- Puzzles every day (chess.com or lichess)
- Opening trainer / repertoire tools (lichess studies)
- Post-game analysis — find where you left theory, where you blundered, one thing to fix
- Occasional coaching; huge step up in club-level play
