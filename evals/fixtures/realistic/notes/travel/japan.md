# Japan

Notes from two trips, mostly Tokyo + Kyoto + Osaka.

## Logistics

- **JR Pass** — worth it for 2+ cities on the same route; not worth it for Tokyo-only
- **Suica / Pasmo** — IC card for subways, buses, vending machines. Add to Apple Wallet.
- **Cash** — still essential. Most restaurants don't take cards. 7-Eleven ATMs take foreign cards.
- **Pocket wifi or eSIM** — offline maps won't cut it, you'll need data

## Tokyo

- **Shibuya / Shinjuku** — crowds, neon, expected
- **Yanaka / Kagurazaka** — old-school neighborhoods, way less touristy
- **Tsukiji outer market** — breakfast sushi before 10am
- **Standing bars in Golden Gai** — tiny, intimate, order the house special
- **TeamLab Planets** — book ahead

## Kyoto

- **Fushimi Inari** — go at 6am to beat crowds, hike to the top (~2 hr)
- **Arashiyama bamboo** — also crowded, also best at dawn
- **Nanzen-ji and the Philosopher's Path** — quieter, walkable
- **Nishiki market** — food stalls, pickles, street-eats

## Osaka

- **Dotonbori** — touristy but iconic, go at night
- **Kuromon Ichiba** — market, lunch spot
- **Day trips** — Nara (deer park), Himeji (castle)

## Food

- **Ichiran / Ippudo** — reliable ramen chains
- **Kaiten-zushi** — conveyor belt sushi, fun + affordable
- **Izakaya** — order small plates, drink highballs
- **Convenience stores are great** — 7-Eleven, Lawson, FamilyMart. Onigiri, egg salad sandwiches, coffee.

## Etiquette

- Shoes off at ryokans, some restaurants
- Quiet on trains — no phone calls
- No tipping (can be insulting)
- Cash in both hands when paying

## What I'd skip

- Robot Restaurant (tourist trap)
- Mega-chain tea ceremonies
- Getting too ambitious with day trips — density is the magic; slow down
