# Portugal

10 days split between Lisbon, Porto, and a countryside stop.

## Lisbon

- **Alfama** — old neighborhood, tiled buildings, fado bars at night
- **LX Factory** — creative district, bookshop (Ler Devagar), cafés
- **Time Out Market** — food hall with the city's best restaurants in stall form
- **Belém** — Jerónimos Monastery, the original pastéis de Belém (worth the line)
- **Trams** — tram 28 is the iconic route, but packed. Tram 12 is the local's alternative.

## Porto

- **Ribeira** — waterfront, bridges, port tastings across the river in Gaia
- **Livraria Lello** — touristy but genuinely beautiful; buy the ticket ahead
- **Port cellars** — Graham's, Taylor's, Kopke all worth a tour
- **Francesinha** — heart-attack sandwich, try it once. Café Santiago is the classic.

## Douro Valley (day trip or overnight)

- **Train from Porto to Pinhão** — stunning ride along the river
- **Quinta tours** — Quinta do Vallado, Quinta Nova — vineyards + tastings + lunch
- **Overnight at a quinta** if you can swing it; the stars are incredible

## Food

- **Pastel de nata** — eat multiple, they're cheap
- **Bacalhau** — salt cod, every restaurant does it differently
- **Sardines** — seasonal (summer), grilled with olive oil and bread
- **Cherries, figs** — in season, from markets, not supermarkets

## Practical

- English widely spoken in cities
- Uber / Bolt work everywhere
- Tip 5-10% if service was good, not required
- Hills are no joke — pack walking shoes

## What I'd do differently

- Less Lisbon, more Porto + north
- Learn 20 Portuguese phrases (pronunciation is nothing like Spanish)
- Book Livraria Lello first thing, not mid-day
