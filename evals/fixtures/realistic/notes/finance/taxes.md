# Taxes

Personal notes, not tax advice.

## Quarterly estimates (1099 / self-employed)

- Due: April 15, June 15, Sept 15, Jan 15
- Safe harbor: pay 100% of last year's tax (110% if income > $150k) to avoid underpayment penalty
- Use IRS Direct Pay online — free, confirmation receipt

## Deductions worth tracking

- Home office (simplified method: $5/sqft up to 300 sqft)
- Health insurance premiums (above-the-line if self-employed)
- Solo 401(k) contributions
- Business expenses (software, books, courses, equipment)
- Mileage (standard rate or actual costs)

## Retirement accounts

- **401(k)** — up to $23k (2024); employer match is free money, take it all
- **Solo 401(k)** — self-employed; up to $69k including employer contrib
- **IRA** — $7k, deductible depending on income
- **Roth IRA** — post-tax, grows tax-free; income limits apply
- **HSA** — triple tax advantage if on HDHP

## Records to keep

- Receipts for 6 years (IRS audit window is 3, but exceptions extend it)
- Keep digital copies (photos, cloud); paper fades
- Year-end summary doc: income by source, deductible categories, carryforwards

## When to get a CPA

- Multiple 1099s + W-2
- Rental properties
- Equity compensation (RSUs, ISOs, NSOs)
- Side businesses with complexity
- Any year you made a big move (relocation, home sale, inheritance)
