# Investing Basics

What I wish someone had told me at 22.

## The boring playbook works

- Index funds (VTI, VXUS, BND) — low fees, broad diversification
- Target-date funds inside retirement accounts — set and forget
- Automate contributions — monthly, first of the month

Most people who try to beat the market underperform the market.

## Account order

1. **401(k) match** — never leave free money
2. **HSA** (if on HDHP) — triple tax advantage
3. **Roth IRA** — tax-free growth
4. **Max 401(k)** — pre-tax or Roth depending on current vs future tax bracket
5. **Brokerage** — long-term growth, flexibility

## Rules of thumb

- Emergency fund first: 3-6 months of expenses in a HYSA
- Stock/bond split by age is outdated — look at risk tolerance and timeline
- "Time in the market beats timing the market" — but only if you can stomach drawdowns
- Low expense ratios matter: 0.05% vs 1% over 40 years is life-changing money

## Taxable accounts

- Hold tax-efficient funds (index ETFs, not actively managed)
- Tax-loss harvesting in down years
- Long-term capital gains (> 1 year hold) = lower rate
- Watch wash sale rule: 30-day window around realizing losses

## What to avoid

- Day trading (tax drag + you'll lose to commissions and spreads)
- Crypto you can't explain
- Options for "fun"
- Financial advisors who charge 1% AUM (flat-fee or hourly only)
