# Budgeting

Budget systems I've tried, what stuck.

## Zero-based budgeting

Every dollar has a job before the month starts. Fine-grained control, high effort. Classic tool: YNAB.

## Anti-budget

Pay yourself first: savings + investments auto-deducted, spend whatever's left guilt-free. Low effort, works once savings rate is healthy.

## 50/30/20

- 50% needs (housing, food, utilities)
- 30% wants (entertainment, dining, hobbies)
- 20% savings and debt payoff

Good starting heuristic for people new to budgeting.

## What I actually do

Hybrid anti-budget:
- Auto-transfer savings/investments on payday (pay yourself first)
- Fixed costs on autopay
- Everything else on a single credit card for tracking
- Review monthly in 10 minutes — not categorized line-by-line

## Tools

- **YNAB** — powerful, pricey, opinionated
- **Copilot** — slick iOS app, good for couples
- **Monarch** — Mint replacement, reasonable
- **Spreadsheet** — free, annoying, educational
- **Bank auto-categorization** — least effort, inconsistent

## The insight

Budget is a plan. Tracking is noticing. You need both — but the exact tool matters less than the habit of looking.
