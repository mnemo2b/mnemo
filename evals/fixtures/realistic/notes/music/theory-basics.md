# Music Theory Basics

Enough theory to learn songs, improvise, and understand what you're doing. Not a degree.

## Notes + intervals

- 12 notes, repeating (A, A#, B, C, C#, D, D#, E, F, F#, G, G#)
- Intervals are distances — 2 semitones = major second, 7 = perfect fifth

## Major scale

Formula: W W H W W W H (whole and half steps)

C major: C D E F G A B (all white keys)

Every other scale is a modification of this.

## Chords

- **Triads** — root, third, fifth
  - Major: 4 + 3 semitones (happy)
  - Minor: 3 + 4 semitones (sad)
  - Diminished: 3 + 3 (tense)
- **Seventh chords** — add a 7th; major 7 is dreamy, dominant 7 wants to resolve

## Key + chord function

In the key of C:
- **I** (C major) — home
- **IV** (F major) — pre-dominant
- **V** (G major) — dominant, wants to resolve to I
- **ii** (D minor), **vi** (A minor) — common substitutions
- **vii°** (B dim) — tension, rarely used by itself

90% of pop songs = I, IV, V, vi in some order.

## Circle of fifths

Organizes keys by how many sharps/flats. Neighbors have one note difference. Opposite sides are maximally distant.

Useful for:
- Modulation (move to a neighboring key smoothly)
- Understanding chord substitutions
- Jazz progressions (often move by fifths: ii-V-I)

## Modes

Scales built from a different starting point of the major scale:
- Ionian (major)
- Dorian (jazz/blues, minor with raised 6th)
- Mixolydian (rock, blues, major with lowered 7th)
- Aeolian (natural minor)

Knowing two or three modes well > memorizing all seven names.
