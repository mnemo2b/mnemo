# Guitar Practice

Current practice structure. 30-45 min/day, 5 days/week.

## Session

- **Warm-up** (5 min) — spider exercise, chromatic runs, slow and clean
- **Technique** (10 min) — one focus area (sweep, bends, alternate picking)
- **Repertoire** (10 min) — song I'm learning, in small sections
- **Improv / ear training** (5-10 min) — over backing tracks
- **Cool down** (5 min) — fingerstyle or just noodling

## Principles

- **Slow is fast** — play it correctly slow, speed comes with repetition
- **Loop the bad bar** — don't play the whole song to get to the part you can't do
- **Record yourself** — the ear lies; recordings don't
- **Focus beats duration** — 20 min of attention > 2 hr of autopilot

## Current focus

- Improvising over minor pentatonic, adding chromatic passing tones
- Hybrid picking (pick + fingers)
- Learning one jazz standard per month (melody + chord comping)

## Tools

- **Metronome** — every practice, every technique segment
- **Looper pedal** — lay down rhythm, solo over it
- **Backing tracks** (YouTube) — for style and feel practice
- **Tab + ear** — use tabs to learn, ears to refine
- **Recorded reference** — play along to the real thing

## Gear (minimal)

- Strat-style electric (swap between single-coil clean and overdriven)
- Small amp or modeler (Fender Tone Master, Kemper, etc.)
- Acoustic for fingerstyle, ear training, and portability
- Tuner, capo, picks (1mm), spare strings

## Keeping motivation

- Have a song you're learning at all times
- Play with other people (online if necessary)
- Bookend practice with something fun, not another scale drill
