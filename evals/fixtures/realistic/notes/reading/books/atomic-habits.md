# Atomic Habits — James Clear

## The 4 laws

Build habits by making them:
1. **Obvious** — cue is clear and visible
2. **Attractive** — tie to something you want
3. **Easy** — reduce friction
4. **Satisfying** — immediate reward, even if small

Break habits by inverting: invisible, unattractive, difficult, unsatisfying.

## Identity over outcomes

Don't focus on "I want to read 50 books this year." Focus on "I am a reader." The identity shift carries the behavior.

Every action is a vote for a type of person. Small wins compound into self-concept.

## Systems over goals

Goals are for direction, systems are for progress. You don't rise to your goals; you fall to your systems.

Two runners with the same goal, different systems (daily short runs vs occasional long ones) end up different places.

## Environment design

Make good habits the path of least resistance. Put the guitar on a stand, not in a case. Put the phone across the room. Keep junk food out of the house.

Willpower is a small, exhaustible resource. Environment runs all day.

## Habit stacking

"After [existing habit], I will [new habit]." Anchors new behavior to automaticity you already have.

## 2-minute rule

Start any new habit with a version so small it can't fail. "Put on workout clothes" eventually becomes a workout. Start too big, abandon before the habit sticks.

## Takeaways I actually apply

- Morning coffee → write 100 words
- Phone charger → moved out of bedroom
- Guitar → on a stand next to the couch
- "Don't miss twice" — one day off is a mistake, two is the start of a pattern
