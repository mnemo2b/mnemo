# 2025 Reading Highlights

Articles and essays I've returned to this year.

## Technical

- **"A Philosophy of Software Design"** — John Ousterhout's deep module principle: interfaces should be simpler than implementation
- **Cal Newport on attention economy** — newsletters, not just Deep Work
- **Will Larson's "Staff Engineer"** — practical framing for ICs past the senior bar

## AI / LLMs

- **Anthropic's engineering blog** — behind the scenes on Claude's training
- **Simon Willison's weblog** — running commentary that consistently clarifies the state of the field
- **"The Bitter Lesson"** (Sutton, 2019, still) — scale beats cleverness
- **"Emergent Abilities of LLMs"** — how capabilities appear at threshold sizes

## Writing

- **Paul Graham essays** — "How to Do Great Work", "The Right Kind of Stubborn"
- **Robin Sloan's newsletter** — writing that respects the reader
- **Maria Popova's marginalia** — quieter, deeper

## Productivity / craft

- **Oliver Burkeman on anti-productivity** — the finitude frame
- **Rick Rubin's "The Creative Act"** (technically a book, but short chapters)

## Ideas I keep chewing on

- **Taste develops before skill** — your ability to discern good work outpaces your ability to produce it. Persist through the gap.
- **Process beats outcome** — control inputs you own; outputs are downstream
- **Distill, don't accumulate** — reading is wasted if nothing gets internalized or shared

## Reading process

- Read widely on weekends, deep in weekdays
- Highlight in Readwise, review weekly
- Write about what I'm learning — forces clarity
- Prune aggressively — unread backlog isn't a debt, it's context that expired
