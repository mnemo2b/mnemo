# Typography

Rules that compound.

## Hierarchy

- Use 2-3 font sizes, not 7
- Weight + size together creates hierarchy (big heavy headlines, small regular body)
- Line height scales with size — bigger text needs less proportional line height

## Measure

- 45-75 characters per line is readable
- Wider → eye fatigues tracking back to the next line
- Narrower → choppy, breaks flow

## Font pairing

- Pair a serif with a sans (contrast), or stay within one superfamily
- Max 2 typefaces. Three tops.
- Test at actual sizes — fonts that look great at 48px can be unreadable at 14px

## Practical defaults

- **Body** — 16-18px, 1.5-1.7 line height
- **Headings** — 1.125-1.25 ratio between levels (h1 > h2 > h3)
- **Letter spacing** — decrease for headings, increase for all-caps
- **Font stack** — always provide system fallbacks

## Common mistakes

- All caps for body copy (unreadable past a sentence)
- Light weights at small sizes (illegible, especially on screens)
- Center-aligned body text (only works for short passages)
- Ignoring contrast (4.5:1 minimum for WCAG AA body text)
