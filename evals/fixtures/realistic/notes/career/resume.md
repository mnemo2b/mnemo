# Resume

What earned interviews over the years.

## Format

- One page for < 10 years of experience, two max after that
- Consistent formatting (dates, titles, locations) beats creative layouts
- ATS-readable — no tables, columns break parsers, plain fonts
- PDF for humans, plain text version available if the portal demands it

## Structure

1. **Header** — name, role, location, links (portfolio, github, linkedin)
2. **Summary** — 2-3 lines, role + specialty + what you're looking for
3. **Experience** — most recent first, 3-5 bullets per role
4. **Projects** — only if they add something experience doesn't
5. **Skills** — keyword-rich for ATS, organized by category
6. **Education** — near the bottom after a few years in industry

## Bullets

Formula: `verb + what + result/scale`.

> Migrated payment system from REST to gRPC, reducing p99 latency by 40% across 200 services.

- Start with verbs (built, led, designed, reduced)
- Concrete numbers beat vague claims
- What YOU did, not what the team did
- Remove adjectives ("innovative", "robust") — show, don't tell

## What to cut

- Objective statements
- Hobbies (unless directly relevant)
- "References available on request"
- Skills you can't defend in an interview
- Jobs > 10-15 years ago unless flagship
