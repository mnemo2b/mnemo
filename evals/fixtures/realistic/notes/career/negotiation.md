# Salary Negotiation

Principles I've picked up. Most from Patrick McKenzie's "Salary Negotiation for Software Developers."

## Before

- **Research comp bands** (Levels.fyi, Blind, community Slacks)
- **Know your walk-away number** — the offer you'd reject
- **Know your ideal number** — what you'd celebrate
- **Multiple offers** (or credible optionality) is leverage

## During the call

- **Don't name a number first** — "I'd like to understand the full comp band" or "what range are you thinking"
- **If forced** — anchor at the top of your researched range
- **Everything is negotiable** — base, equity, bonus, sign-on, start date, title

## After an offer

- **Don't accept immediately** — "Thanks, I'd like to review and get back to you in a few days"
- **Counter** — name a specific number with a brief rationale
- **Be specific** — "I'd be comfortable at $X base" beats "a bit more"
- **Multiple rounds are normal** — don't cave in round 1

## Language

- "I'm excited about this opportunity" + ask = collaborative, not adversarial
- "What flexibility is there on…" — opens doors without demanding
- "Can you help me get to…" — frames the recruiter as your ally

## Don't

- Lie about competing offers
- Threaten to walk if you won't
- Get emotional in writing
- Accept before negotiating, even if the first offer feels great
