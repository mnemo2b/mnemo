# Interview Prep

What I do before interviews. YMMV; tuned for senior IC roles.

## Company research

- Read the "about" page, recent blog posts, press releases
- Skim product docs / feature launches
- Glassdoor / Blind for culture and comp signals
- LinkedIn: who are my interviewers, what did they work on before

Walk in with 3-5 thoughtful questions about the team, product, or tech.

## Behavioral

- 5-7 stories in your back pocket that demonstrate:
  - Leadership without authority
  - Disagreement handling
  - Ambiguity / uncertainty
  - Impact / results
  - Failure / learning
- STAR format (Situation, Task, Action, Result)
- Keep stories tight — 2-3 minutes, ready to expand on ask

## System design

- Draw the architecture, don't just talk
- Clarify requirements first (scale, consistency, latency)
- Start simple, iterate — don't design for 100M users out of the gate
- Trade-offs, not absolutes ("X gives us Y at the cost of Z")

## Coding

- Think aloud — interviewers grade process, not just answers
- Ask clarifying questions before coding
- Write test cases before (or alongside) the code
- Complexity analysis unprompted

## Day-of

- Sleep > review
- Water, bathroom, snack before blocks of back-to-back interviews
- Decompress between sessions — 5 min of nothing

## After

- Send a thank-you within 24 hr referencing specific conversation points
- Note what went well / poorly in a doc — compounds over time
