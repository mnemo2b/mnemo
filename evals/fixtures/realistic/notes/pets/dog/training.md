# Dog Training

Positive reinforcement, clicker-based. Current dog is a 3-year-old rescue mix.

## Core principles

- **Reward what you want, ignore what you don't** — attention reinforces behavior, even negative attention
- **Timing** — reward within 1 second of the behavior
- **Consistency** — everyone in the household uses same cues
- **Short sessions** — 5-10 min, 2-3x/day
- **End on success** — one more easy win before stopping

## Foundation cues

Train in this order, move on only when each is solid:

1. Name (eye contact on name)
2. Sit
3. Down
4. Stay (duration, distance, distraction — add one at a time)
5. Come / recall
6. Leave it / drop it
7. Loose leash walking
8. Place (go to bed/mat)

## Recall

The most important and hardest cue. Rules:
- NEVER call to punish / end fun
- Always pay (high-value treats, every time, for months)
- Practice starting at 1 foot away, slowly increase distance and distractions
- Different word for emergency recall, trained fresh, used rarely

## Common mistakes

- Repeating cues (say "sit" once, wait — don't escalate to "sit, sit, SIT")
- Punishing after the fact (dog can't connect)
- Expecting house-wide behavior after training in the kitchen — generalize gradually
- Skipping cool-down after excitement (fetch, walks) — dogs need to learn to settle

## Gear

- 6' flat leash for walks
- Long line (15-30') for recall practice in open areas
- Treat pouch (keeps treats accessible, reinforces timing)
- Kong / snuffle mat / lickmat for enrichment and settling
