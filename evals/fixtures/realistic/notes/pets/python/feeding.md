# Python Feeding

Ball python feeding schedule and troubleshooting. Mine is a 3-year-old female, ~1500g.

## Schedule

- **Adults** (>1000g) — one medium/large rat every 14 days
- **Juveniles** — one small rat every 7-10 days
- **Hatchlings** — one fuzzy/hopper every 5-7 days

Overfeeding is common and leads to obesity. Err toward less frequent, appropriately sized prey.

## Prey size

Prey should be ~10-15% of the snake's body weight. Alternatively, prey diameter ≈ 1-1.5x the snake's widest girth.

- ~1500g snake = ~150g rat = medium rat

## Frozen-thawed vs live

- **Frozen-thawed** is safer — no risk of prey biting the snake back
- Thaw in fridge overnight, then warm in hot (not boiling) water 15-20 min
- Offer with feeding tongs — never fingers
- Live only as a last resort if the snake refuses F/T

## Refusals

Ball pythons fast. A healthy adult can go months without eating during:
- **Breeding season** (fall/winter)
- **Shed cycle** (eyes go blue, eat later)
- **Temperature drops** (check thermostat)
- **Stress** (new enclosure, handling, disturbance)

Don't panic over a single refused meal. Weigh weekly; intervene if > 10% body weight loss.

## Feeding response

Good feeding response: coiled, alert, strikes when prey is near. Poor response: no interest, head ducks. Common causes: too warm (no hide), shed, stress, too large prey.

## Notes

- Feed in enclosure is fine — the "feeding tub" myth is debunked
- Don't handle for 48 hr after a meal (regurgitation risk)
- Mine prefers rats dangled, head-first, in low light
