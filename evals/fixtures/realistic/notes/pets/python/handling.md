# Python Handling

How to read the snake and not create a stressed animal.

## When to handle

- **Not during shed** — eyes go blue and cloudy; visibility is low, defensive response up
- **Not within 48 hr of feeding** — regurgitation risk
- **Not in a new environment** — give at least 2 weeks to settle before first handle
- **Short sessions** — 10-15 min max, a few times per week is plenty

## Reading body language

- **Curled ball** — scared or resting, don't force
- **S-curve neck, hissing** — back off, respect the warning
- **Tongue flicks** — exploring, curious
- **Slow, smooth movement** — calm, comfortable

## Pickup technique

- Approach from the side, not above (above = predator)
- Support midbody with both hands
- Never grab behind the head — use gentle redirection
- Let the snake move through your hands, don't constrict or pin

## Defensive behaviors

- **Balling up** — ball pythons' namesake stress response
- **Musking** — foul-smelling discharge; usually a sign you've pushed too far
- **Bite** — rare, almost always defensive, doesn't mean much for a non-venomous snake

## Safety

- **No handling alone for the first few months** — with a new snake, always have a spotter
- **Wash hands before and after** — smell of prey triggers feeding response
- **Never handle after handling rodents** — same reason
- **Kids need supervision** — not because of the snake, because of the kids

## If bitten

- Don't pull away — you'll tear your skin and the snake's teeth
- Gentle pressure or cold water usually convinces them to release
- Clean with soap and water; bites from a non-venomous snake are low risk
