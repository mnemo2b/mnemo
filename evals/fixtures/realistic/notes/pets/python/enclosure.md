# Python Enclosure

Current setup for a ~1500g ball python.

## Dimensions

- 4' x 2' x 2' PVC enclosure (Animal Plastics T8)
- Opaque sides reduce stress
- Sliding front access

Ball pythons are terrestrial and need floor space more than height. Minimum for adult: 4 sq ft.

## Heating

- **Warm side** — 88-92°F surface temp, achieved with a radiant heat panel (RHP)
- **Cool side** — 75-80°F
- **Ambient** — 78-82°F

- Thermostat is NON-NEGOTIABLE. Herpstat or VE-200 recommended.
- Never use heat rocks (burn hazard)
- Halogen flood bulbs or RHPs > coil-style ceramic heaters

## Humidity

- **Ambient** — 55-65%
- **Shed time** — bump to 70%+ via humid hide

- Digital hygrometer, not analog (analog reads wrong and moldy)
- Spray mist 2-3x/week if dry climate, daily during shed

## Hides

- **Warm hide** — essential
- **Cool hide** — essential
- **Humid hide** — moist sphagnum moss, especially during shed

Hides should be snug — the snake should fill them, not rattle around.

## Substrate

- Cypress mulch or aspen shavings
- Coco husk for higher humidity
- Avoid pine and cedar (toxic oils)

## Water

- Bowl large enough to soak in, cool side
- Change weekly, deep clean (dish soap) monthly

## Enrichment

- Branches for climbing (they do climb, despite being terrestrial)
- Foliage for cover
- Variety in layout — rearrange quarterly
