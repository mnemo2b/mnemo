# Moving

What I've learned across four moves.

## 6 weeks out

- Declutter aggressively — paying to move things you don't want is dumb
- Book movers (or truck rental). Weekends and month-ends book up.
- Change of address at USPS, start the 30-day forwarding
- Update banks, subscriptions, DMV, employer, insurance

## 4 weeks out

- Gather supplies: boxes (many), packing paper, bubble wrap, sharpies, tape
- Start packing rarely-used rooms (attic, closets, bookshelves)
- Label boxes with room + contents, not just room

## 1 week out

- Pack everything except essentials (1 week of clothes, toiletries, dishes for 2-3 meals)
- Take photos of electronics wiring before disconnecting
- Defrost freezer, drain gas from mowers

## Day before

- Pack an "essentials" box: toilet paper, soap, snacks, phone chargers, tools, basic cookware
- Pack an overnight bag
- Confirm mover arrival time

## Moving day

- Be there or designate someone
- Walk through old place before leaving — check behind doors, under sinks, in drawers
- Check inventory as movers unload
- Tip movers (cash, 10-20% or flat $20-40 per person for a full-day move)

## First week in new place

- Find the water shut-off, electrical panel
- Change locks or rekey
- Test smoke detectors
- One room at a time — resist the urge to unpack everything at once

## Tips that compound

- Clothes in garbage bags over hangers — costs you minutes, saves boxes
- Dishes wrapped in clothes/towels — double duty
- Books go in small boxes or you'll regret it
