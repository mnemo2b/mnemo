# Editorial Principles

What goes live, what stays in drafts.

## Publish when

- You've changed your own mind writing it (indicates new thinking)
- The idea is specific enough to be wrong
- The first draft scared you a little
- You've given it at least 48 hrs between drafts

## Don't publish when

- You're still processing the event emotionally
- The post is a reaction to someone else's post (usually)
- It's a list of complaints without a frame
- You're uncertain about more than one of: thesis, audience, action the reader should take

## Piece types

- **Argumentative** — lead with the claim, defend with evidence
- **Explainer** — lead with the problem, walk through the resolution
- **Personal essay** — lead with the specific moment, generalize at the end
- **Notebook / marginalia** — fragment, quote, short reaction; low stakes

Know which you're writing before drafting.

## Iteration

- First draft: get it down, one sitting
- Second draft: structure + cut
- Third draft: sentence-by-sentence polish
- Read aloud before publishing

## Publishing cadence

- Consistency > frequency — weekly newsletters beat random monthly
- Don't ship broken things — better to delay than to post a mess
- Old posts get visitors forever — favor evergreen framing over news
- Promote published work; writing in public requires distribution

## What I've learned

- Being useful beats being brilliant
- Audience is found, not announced — write for one reader
- The second piece on a topic is usually better than the first
- Editing is writing; the best writers cut ruthlessly
