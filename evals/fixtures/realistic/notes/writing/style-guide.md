# Writing Style Guide

Personal defaults for everything I publish.

## Voice

- **Direct** — state the thing, then elaborate
- **Concrete** — specific examples over abstract claims
- **Quiet** — authority without shouting
- **Conversational** — contractions, varied sentence length

## Don't

- **Hedging language** — "perhaps", "might", "I think" when you know
- **Empty intensifiers** — "very", "really", "extremely"
- **Emdashes everywhere** — overused as a crutch for run-ons
- **Ellipses in prose** — trailing off is almost always avoidable
- **"Delve", "leverage", "unlock"** — business-school vocab
- **Throat-clearing openers** — "In this article, we will..."

## Do

- **Show, don't tell** — describe the specific moment, not the general lesson
- **Active voice** — "the team shipped" > "was shipped by the team"
- **One idea per sentence** — split long sentences even when grammatically correct
- **Cut transitions** — "however", "furthermore", "additionally" usually signal weakness in flow

## Structure

- **Lead with the claim** — don't bury the point
- **Short paragraphs** — 3-5 sentences max
- **Headers that describe** — not "Introduction", but "Why this matters"
- **Conclude or stop** — never both

## Titles

- Specific > clever
- Promise a concrete payoff
- Numbers and nouns are sticky
- Avoid clickbait; readers can smell it

## Before publishing

- Read aloud — awkwardness surfaces
- Cut 10% — it's always too long on first draft
- Check for unnecessary qualifiers
- Does the first sentence earn the second?
